The World's Most Beautiful Train Journey: Ella to Kandy

By **Samantha Perera** November 10, 2025 8 min read

There are train rides, and then there is **The Train Ride**. Winding through the central highlands of Sri Lanka, the journey from Ella to Kandy (or vice-versa) is often hailed as one of the most breathtaking rail routes on the planet. Itâ€™s a slow, seven hour immersion into an emerald world of misty mountains, cascading waterfalls, and endless, manicured tea plantations.

The Climb to Nanu Oya: A Green Sea

The first hours leaving Ella are a symphony of greens. You pass famous landmarks like the **Nine Arch Bridge**, where the train slows down just enough for you to capture that perfect, postcard-worthy photo. Make sure to grab a second or third-class ticket for the best window views (or lack thereof, since many windows are open!).

The air is cool, carrying the scent of damp earth and fresh tea leaves. Every window is a living, moving landscape painting.

- A fellow traveler on the train.

Tea Estates and Local Life

As the train gains altitude towards **Nuwara Eliya** (via Nanu Oya station), the climate noticeably shifts. You'll see Tamil tea-pluckers, vibrant in their saris, dotting the impossibly steep hillsides. This is the heart of Ceylon Tea country, a legacy left by colonial times that still thrives today.

Tea pluckers are a common, beautiful sight along the route.

Arrival in Kandy: Cultural Capital

The final approach into Kandy, Sri Lanka's cultural capital, is a gentle descent back into a warmer, more bustling environment. You leave the quiet serenity of the mountains for the historic charm of Kandy Lake and the **Temple of the Tooth Relic**. The train ride is an adventure, but Kandy is the perfect place to settle down and reflect on the beauty you just witnessed.
